How to use this site

Practical answers for the inventory map, most-asked first. This page is about driving the site; the science behind the data — classification, rules, areas and volumes — lives in Methods.

What do the dots and colors mean?

  • Catastrophic landslides are blues, darkest for the most recent (2012+), lighter for Modern (1850–2011), lightest for Holocene. A colored ring around a catastrophic dot records precursory creep evidence.
  • Slow-moving landslides are red / yellow / green by strength of movement evidence: red = obvious creep, yellow = subtle (e.g. InSAR-detected), green = geomorphic evidence only. A yellow dot with a red center = patchy obvious creep.
  • Magenta = a record still missing the key attribute its display depends on (under review).
  • Half-size dots are features below the inventory's size thresholds; they carry the same colors.
  • The full clickable legend, with per-class counts, is the Inventory tab of the sidebar. Zoom in and the dots gain their mapped outlines (source, body, deposit polygons).

How do I look at a specific landslide?

  • Click any dot or polygon: the panel shows its attributes, evidence, imagery links, and — for many recent events — an embedded Planet satellite timelapse.
  • Double-click a landslide to jump to its curated best view (framing, imagery, sometimes a before/after wiper). The ⌖ Default view button in the panel does the same.
  • The record's name is a permalink — right-click it and copy to share a URL that opens the map right there.

How do I change the imagery, or compare two eras?

  • Switch basemaps with the dropdown at the top of the sidebar, or browse thumbnails in the Reference maps tab — including 1978–86 AHAP aerial photography and historic topo.
  • For side-by-side comparison, click ＋ Add wiper (Compare section of Reference maps). A draggable ⇄ divider splits the map, and a panel appears on the right: the sidebar controls the left side, the panel controls the right — pick each side's basemap and overlays independently. E.g. AHAP left vs modern satellite right to see decades of change. Landslide data reads continuously across the divider; untick classes for a clean image-only wipe.
  • Overlays: drape USGS landslide susceptibility or OPERA InSAR velocity (ascending / descending) semi-transparently over the basemap, with an opacity slider — in the sidebar for the map (left side while wiping), in the wiper's panel for the right side. E.g. the same imagery on both sides with ascending velocity on one and descending on the other, to check whether a slope's motion is real or a look-direction artifact. A "None (blank white)" basemap is available when you want an overlay on a clean canvas.
  • Each landslide's panel also links out to external imagery browsers (ESRI Wayback time series, Sentinel, OPERA InSAR, historic topo).

How do I share exactly what I'm seeing?

Copy the address bar — the URL always encodes your center, zoom, basemap, and any active wiper (with its divider position). For a single landslide, prefer its name-permalink; that link survives map changes and opens at the record's curated view.

How do I filter what's shown?

  • Type and class checkboxes: pinned strip + Inventory tab; counts update live.
  • Dual-handle sliders (Inventory tab) filter by source/deposit area, volume, age, and modeled susceptibility.
  • Limit to map view restricts the counts and the analysis charts to the current screen.

How do I explore timing and statistics?

The Analysis tab opens floating panels: the seasonal histogram, the time series, and the susceptibility scatter — on the scatter, drag a box to filter the map to that range (two-way synced with the sliders).

How do I get the data?

⬇ Download in the header: the full inventory as GeoJSON with QGIS styles and a manifest (formats, conventions, rules, attribution). Drag into QGIS and the styling auto-applies. See Methods §1 for the format.

Small conveniences

  • Right-click (Ctrl-click on Mac) anywhere on the map copies the coordinates to your clipboard.
  • Leaving the map to another page and returning restores the view you left.

For data admins

Visible to inventory editors only — most-common tasks first.

How do I edit a record?

  • Click the ⚙ gear in its map panel (or go through Manage). Scalar fields autosave on blur (blue ✓); dates accept 14-Sep-2010 or ISO; Save changes is only for subset checkboxes and the polygon role table.
  • Links in description / notes: write [text](https://…), or just paste text copied from a web page — links convert automatically and render clickable on the map.
  • On catastrophic records, tick Creep evaluated to reveal the creep-evidence fields; the read-only OPERA links beside insar_opera open the InSAR viewer at the site.

How do I add a new landslide?

  • ✏ draw (top-left on the map) → click vertices → Enter to finish, Esc to cancel → name + role (source / body / deposit).
  • Same name = same landslide: stage several polygons under one name to build one record; an existing name attaches the polygon to that record (refused if it's >~5 km away — use a distinct name).
  • Teal drafts survive reloads; Commit creates pending (magenta, editor-only) records and opens review.

How do I fix existing geometry?

In the record's form, ✎ Edit geometry on the preview map: drag vertices, add/delete polygons, Save. History is kept, derived fields recompute, and the primary polygon re-normalizes automatically (catastrophic → one source; slow → body) — the role table's radio is the manual override.

How do I trace from my own imagery (GeoTIFF)?

  • Reference maps → Traced imagery (uploads) → ＋ Upload GeoTIFF. Requirements: a georeferenced GeoTIFF (embedded CRS; any projection). Give it a title, the image capture date (dating evidence), and a source note.
  • After the bake (seconds–minutes) the image appears under the data layers; dim it with its opacity slider and trace with ✏.
  • Afterwards, with the landslide's panel open, click ⚲ link on the upload row — the record's panel then offers a Traced from card that restores the image in one click.
  • Uploads are admin-only and stay out of the wiper's comparison pane, so you can wipe between your image and any basemap.

How do I set how a landslide is first seen?

Frame the map (center, zoom, basemap, wiper) → open the record's panel → Set default view. Slug links, permalinks, and double-click use it from then on. Frame public records over public basemaps — trace uploads aren't visible to visitors.

How do I add a Planet animation?

Paste the planet.com/stories/… URL into planet_story_link and save. Classification, archiving, and the in-panel embed happen automatically within a minute or so.

How do I work through flagged records?

Scans (naming, undated catastrophics, weak timing, broken encodings) set ⚑ flags with reasons. On the map: filter to Flagged, pin a field under Map labels, click through, fix inline, and Clear flag.

How do subsets work now?

  • Tags (e.g. alaska-2025) are hand-checked on the edit form; snapshotted tags are frozen.
  • Regions (alaska, kim, schaefer, higman) compute membership from the primary-polygon centroid — the form chips are read-only and follow the geometry. Region polygons are replaced via load_region_geometry (ops docs).

How do I bulk import or export?

Manage → ⬆ Import (GeoJSON zip, .gpkg, .shp, .kml) shows a preview diff — adds, updates, collisions — before anything is applied; new records enter review. ⬇ Export round-trips through QGIS byte-stably.
